# Future Citizen Wallet Blueprint Notes

Source:
- `/Users/kyusdaison/Downloads/Future_Citizen_Blueprint.pdf`

## Core Judgment

Future Citizen is not positioned as only a public chain project.
It is described as a higher-level digital infrastructure system for future citizens, centered on:

- Identity
- Trust
- Governance

The wallet therefore should not be designed as a standalone crypto wallet only.
Its correct role is a citizen-facing entry point that connects identity, assets, verification, and interaction.

## System Structure

The blueprint defines a three-layer architecture:

1. `Future Citizen Bureau`
   - The initiating institution and parent brand
   - Responsible for system launch, institutional design, and ecosystem operation

2. `Future Citizen Authority`
   - The governance and trust framework
   - Responsible for identity governance, rule logic, certification mechanisms, and ecosystem coordination

3. `Future Citizen Chain`
   - The technical base layer
   - Responsible for blockchain infrastructure, on-chain records, value interaction, identity verification, and settlement support

## Three Types of Trust

The whole system is designed to reconstruct trust in three dimensions:

- Organizational trust: `Bureau`
- Governance trust: `Authority`
- Technical trust: `Chain`

For wallet design, this means the product should communicate more than asset custody.
It should also express legitimacy, rule clarity, and infrastructure reliability.

## Application Layer Matrix

Around `Future Citizen Core`, the blueprint lists these application-layer extensions:

- `Future Citizen ID`
  - Digital identity entry and carrier

- `Future Citizen Wallet`
  - Connects identity, assets, and interaction

- `Verification Network`
  - Provides certification and trusted verification capability

- `Governance Tools`
  - Supports rule execution and coordination

- `Digital Residency Layer`
  - Carries cross-border ecosystem access

This implies the wallet is a hub product, not an isolated tool.

## Official Communication Rules

The blueprint sets a recommended communication order:

1. Introduce the institution first: `Future Citizen Bureau`
2. Then explain the governance framework: `Future Citizen Authority`
3. Then explain the technical base: `Future Citizen Chain`

Two explicit red lines:

- Do not reduce the brand into "a public chain project"
- Do not explain `Bureau` and `Authority` as two parallel duplicated institutions

## Wallet Design Implications

When designing the Future Citizen Wallet, it should be framed as:

- A citizen portal, not just a token wallet
- A bridge between identity, assets, and trusted actions
- A front-end container for trust, verification, and participation
- Part of the Future Citizen system, not an independent product narrative

## Suggested Wallet Capability Directions

Based on the blueprint alone, the wallet likely needs to support these directions:

- Identity onboarding and citizen profile
- Credential display and verification
- Asset account and transaction views
- Trusted interaction flows
- Governance participation or governance-related notices
- Residency or ecosystem access entry points

## Design Principles For Next Step

When the detailed wallet content arrives, the design should follow these principles:

- Lead with identity before pure finance
- Keep trust and verification visible, not hidden in settings
- Present assets as one module inside a broader citizen system
- Show connections to the wider ecosystem
- Use language that feels institutional, trustworthy, and future-facing

## Added Context From Product Artifact Summary

From the new material provided in chat, the wallet scope now appears more concrete.
The product is not only a citizen identity wallet, but a multi-layer gateway that combines identity, payments, recovery, enterprise access, physical linkage, and secure communication.

The six stated core modules are:

- `FCDID` and privacy credentials
- `MPC`-based seedless account recovery
- Automatic gas conversion and invisible stablecoin payment
- `E-Company` gateway for digital enterprise use cases
- Offline `NFC` physical card linkage
- Encrypted communication layer based on `DIDComm`

## Interpreted Product Positioning

Based on the artifact summary, `FC Wallet` should be treated as:

- A trusted citizen wallet
- A privacy-preserving identity wallet
- A seamless payment wallet
- A gateway to enterprise and institutional participation
- A bridge between digital identity and physical credentials
- A secure communication container

This strengthens the earlier judgment that the wallet is not just a crypto asset tool.
It is closer to a full-stack operating interface for future citizens.

## Capability Implications By Module

### FCDID and Privacy Credentials

Design likely needs:

- Identity dashboard
- Credential vault
- Proof request review flow
- Selective disclosure interactions
- Privacy status and trust indicators

### MPC Recovery

Design likely needs:

- Seedless onboarding explanation
- Guardian or shard-based recovery flows
- Recovery status visibility
- Human-readable safety explanations
- Account recovery rehearsal or education screens

### Auto Gas Conversion and Invisible Stablecoin Payment

Design likely needs:

- Payment confirmation screens that hide blockchain complexity
- Clear display of spend amount, settlement path, and final received value
- Auto gas handling without requiring the user to manage gas manually
- Stablecoin-first payment language

### E-Company Gateway

Design likely needs:

- Enterprise identity or entity profile
- Corporate wallet switching or workspace context
- Multi-role permission management
- Multi-sign approval or execution flows
- Separation between personal citizen actions and organizational actions

### NFC Physical Card Linkage

Design likely needs:

- Physical card binding flow
- Card status view
- NFC tap actions
- Lost-card / freeze / rebind flows
- Online-offline trust handoff screens

### DIDComm Communication Layer

Design likely needs:

- Secure inbox or message center
- Verified sender indicators
- Credential request messages
- Governance or enterprise notices
- Privacy-first chat or exchange confirmation patterns
